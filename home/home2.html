Our Identity Today

In Ghana now, most locals of IMCS are born in already existing chaplaincies where many other subgroups or organizations are at work. Subgroups such as Charismatic Renewal group, Legion of Mary, Choir, different Marian groups came into existence after these IMCS Locals were formed in the chaplaincies. IMCS does embrace all the Catholic Student Communities and it represents them. As a result of the quasi parish nature of the chaplaincies, the activities of IMCS locals sometimes have activities like, retreat, Mass and programs like campaigns against HIV/AIDS, Sickle Cell Awareness, community Empowerment and outreaches. This awareness was felt by action orientations that became more and more concise on some of our campuses. In furtherance to this, the National Office would be seeking the counsel and support of the Ghana Catholic Bishop Conference on and in what the National Movement’s locals or institutions do in the Church in Ghana,

The University Pastoral Work

As IMCS grew over the years and adapted itself to the realities it was faced, in Ghana, with the growth of the Church’s pastoral activities in universities. In some mission countries, many Catholic Universities were established and the concern to foster spiritual care for the university community (Lectures, administration and the families) was kindled. University parishes exercising full parish responsibilities and having organizations were founded; this meant community of Catholic Students had then parish committees. All movements and organizations recognized by the parish found their place in it. The situation is not farfetched in Ghana.

In Ghana for non-Catholic universities, the concern to carter for the spiritual needs of the students was met within the creation of Catholic Chaplaincies. Often, a movement particularly IMCS, became the reason for establishing a chaplaincy which in turn became representative of the Catholic Students Community. As time went on these University Chaplaincies developed and became known as University Parishes by Local Ordinaries or Bishops. With the change from one to another, some tensions have been encountered and IMCS humbly had to give up its place to a higher authority and the personality of University Parishes had a determining effect on the transition and performance of the IMCS Movement activities. Currently, many students feel they are Catholics but not IMCS-Pax Romana members in these Parish Chaplaincies. That is, the IMCS Movement has now become a movement of choice by the Catholic Students in these Parish Chaplaincies. Pax Romana in the Tertiary institutions is virtually becoming optional society for the Catholic Students due to wrong orientation. This has led to the lack of smooth relationship between some locals with their Chaplains and gradually resulted in their participation in some of the National Programs.

IMCS Ghana Federation Coordination

The basic logistics such as an office, human and financial recourses needed to make administrative work smooth is nothing to write home about, coupled with our inability to create a transitional structure for the graduates to form a strong Alumni (ICMICA) front in the country for some years now and our inability to permeate the Arch/Diocese structure which in turn will trickle down to deaneries and to the parish levels has militated our coordination and adversely affected our financial status. The slow or non- payment of dues by locals cannot be overlooked. The non-permanent places of worship in some of our locals have always been a long standing issue. The National Office has developed a ten year Action Plan for the Movement 2014-2024, which will see to the establishment of PAX ALUMNI in our various locals to strengthen the emerging national front, start a long term income generating project-Cloth Production to end the period of “intellectual begging”, acquiring an office through the help of the Bishops’ Conference and GHANCYC and ensuring financial sustainability and self-reliance.
